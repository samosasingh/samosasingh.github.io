Samosa Story, How Samosa’s came to India?

  • The capital of Akbar’s empire has a very famous monument by the name of “SAMOSA MAHAL.” Samosa Mahal is so known because of its triangular shape that resembles the shape of an Indian snack known as 'samosa'. It is said to be the residence of one of the nobles of Akbar.
  • Sunita Williams took Samosas with her to space.
  • Due to its overwhelming popularity, Samosa is conferred the title “The King of Indian Snacks”.
  • The Samosa is a truly international food which is enjoyed by millions around the globe
  • Samosa is one of the most popular Indian snacks in USA, UK, and other European Countries.
  • Samosas are healthier than you think. An average Samosa has less than 200 calories and just less than 20 grams of fat and over 30 grams of carbohydrates.
  • Vegetarian samosas originated in Uttar Pradesh.
  • The largest samosa is 110.8 kg (244 lb 4 oz) and was created by Bradford College (UK), in Bradford, UK, on 22 June 2012. The samosa measured 135 cm long (53 in), 85 cm wide (33 in) and 29 cm high (11 in).
  • Samosa has about 47 different names and about 1000 different varieties in terms of size, shape, fillings, texture etc.
  • The samosa is the most delicious little immigrant South Asia has ever seen!
